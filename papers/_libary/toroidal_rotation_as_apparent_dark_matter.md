### 3. THE RELATIVITY ARGUMENT

The equivalence principle (in PHYS-1's framing: inertia = inertia, tautology):

2. Centripetal acceleration: material in circular motion requires centripetal force. From the outside, this looks like additional gravitational attraction toward the center of curvature. For the toroidal flow, the center of curvature is the major axis of the torus — the galactic center for a galactic disk torus.

3. The virial theorem: for a self-gravitating system in equilibrium, 2K + U = 0 where K is kinetic energy and U is gravitational potential energy. The kinetic energy of the circulation ADDS to the total energy budget. An observer measuring the total gravitational effect measures K + U, not U alone. The circulation energy K appears as additional gravitational mass.

From the co-moving observer's perspective: all forces balance, everything is in equilibrium, the circulation is just "how things move here." No dark matter needed to explain local dynamics.

From the external observer's perspective (or from the perspective of measuring the total gravitational effect of the system): the circulation adds energy, energy has inertia, inertia curves spacetime, the curvature appears as additional gravity attributed to unseen mass.

### 4. GALAXY ROTATION CURVES

The flat rotation curve: stars at large galactic radii orbit at constant velocity instead of declining as v ∝ 1/√r (Keplerian). The standard explanation: dark matter halo provides additional mass M(r) ∝ r, giving v = constant.

The toroidal rotation explanation: the galactic disk is a toroidal soliton. The material circulates poloidally (through the disk cross-section) in addition to orbiting toroidally (around the galactic center). The poloidal circulation is the "vertical" component of stellar motion — stars oscillating above and below the disk midplane as they orbit. This oscillation IS a circulation through the disk cross-section.

The observed vertical oscillation of stars in the Milky Way disk has a period of ~60–80 Myr and amplitude of ~100–300 pc. This is the poloidal circulation. Its energy contributes to the disk's gravitational budget.

At large radii, the disk becomes thicker (the scale height increases). The poloidal circulation amplitude increases. The energy of the circulation increases. The effective inertia from the circulation increases. The measured rotation velocity stays constant because the increasing circulation energy compensates for the decreasing visible mass density.

The flat rotation curve is not evidence for dark matter substance. It is evidence that the toroidal soliton's circulation energy increases with radius in exactly the way needed to keep v constant. Whether this is a coincidence or a structural property of the toroidal soliton (its mode spectrum, from Chapter 6 of the super notebook) is the research question.

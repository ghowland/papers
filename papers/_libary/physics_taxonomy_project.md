# Project Notebook: The Physics Taxonomy Migration

**Status:** Future project. Begins after RUM book release, YouTube series, and distributed database establish the foundation.

**Purpose:** Replace physics's historical naming system with a structural taxonomy that makes the entire field navigable without requiring years of vocabulary memorization.

---

## Mission Statement

Physics is two subjects joined at the hip: the actual physics (measurements, equations, derivations) and the accumulated historical vocabulary from every naming event since the 1800s. You must pass both to participate. The vocabulary layer functions as a selection filter that gates entry to the field, filtering out people with the conceptual capacity but not the patience for arbitrary authority.

This project proposes to collapse that filter by providing structural alternatives, historical lexicon mapping, and pedagogical tools that make the field accessible in weeks rather than years.

The historical names stay. The structural names are added. Both coexist. The structure wins by being useful.

---

## The Four Deliverables

### Deliverable 1: The Structural Map

**What it is:** The canonical reference. Every soliton, every level, every property, every relationship in one navigable hierarchy. The atlas.

**Audience:** People entering fresh. Engineers crossing over. Motivated amateurs. Students who haven't yet been imprinted with the historical vocabulary.

**Format:** Database + printed reference + interactive visualization. The taxonomy as the primary key. Nodes for every soliton at every level. Edges for nesting, derivation, coupling. Inertia and reading values as node properties. Zoomable from cosmic to vacuum scale.

**Success criterion:** A 12-year-old with the map can locate any particle, any structure, any interaction in the physics hierarchy and know its essential properties without consulting a glossary.

---

### Deliverable 2: The Lexicon Mapper

**What it is:** The migration tool. Every historical name resolves to a taxonomy path. Every taxonomy path lists its historical aliases.

**Audience:** Credentialed physicists who want to gain structural navigation without abandoning their existing fluency. Science journalists trying to translate between registers. Cross-field researchers.

**Format:** Bidirectional lookup. Historical → structural (e.g., "Weinberg angle → sin²θ_W → Electroweak boundary reading, dimensionless, derived from α and α_s at M_Z"). Structural → historical (e.g., "Confinement.Composite-Pair → historical aliases: meson, pion, kaon, D meson, B meson, etc. with their discovery dates and significance").

**Why this matters:** If the mapper only goes structural → historical, the institution can dismiss it. If it goes both directions, the institution's own vocabulary becomes navigable through the taxonomy. They cannot reject the mapper without rejecting access to their own names. This is the protective layer.

**Success criterion:** A physicist can use the historical vocabulary they know and the taxonomy interchangeably, with seamless translation in both directions.

---

### Deliverable 3: The Easy Study Guide

**What it is:** Pedagogical delivery. "Learn N words for the 1 you know that works everywhere."

**Audience:** Self-learners, students frustrated with conventional curricula, engineers who want physics competence without a physics degree.

**Format:** Start with the structural map. Give the student lifetime navigation of physics in six weeks. Then layer the historical names on top as optional decoration — performative fluency for when you want to sound fancy in traditional contexts.

**The inversion:** Current pedagogy forces students to memorize names before understanding structure. This guide teaches structure first, then attaches names to a structure the student already understands. Names become easier, not harder, because they have a place to attach.

**Example passage:** "You now understand that the Electroweak boundary has a reading, dimensionless, that describes how the weak and electromagnetic forces mix. In traditional physics this reading is called the 'Weinberg angle' and written sin²θ_W. Steven Weinberg published the paper in 1967 that earned the eponym. If you want to sound fluent, say 'Weinberg angle.' If you want to be understood, say 'electroweak mixing reading.' Both are correct. Both refer to the same thing."

**Success criterion:** Six weeks of self-study produces a reader who can locate any physics concept in the structural map, recognize the historical names attached to each concept, and read physics papers fluently despite having no formal training.

---

### Deliverable 4: The Cultural Intervention

**What it is:** Public distribution and advocacy. The taxonomy becomes a thing people know exists and can use.

**Audience:** The culture at large. Educators. Science communicators. Curious people who never studied physics formally.

**Format:** Open publication (Zenodo, GitHub, Amazon). YouTube explainers. Collaborative distributed database (fork-compute-merge). Lectures. Workshops. Open curricula. Community-contributed translations into other languages.

**Success criterion:** Ten years after release, a significant fraction of new physics learners start with the taxonomy. Physics departments begin teaching structural naming alongside historical naming. The filter effect weakens. More diverse people enter physics. Progress accelerates because selection is no longer based on vocabulary tolerance.

## Why This Matters Beyond Physics

Every scientific field with accumulated historical vocabulary has the same disease. Chemistry mostly cleaned it up (periodic table). Biology mostly cleaned it up (Linnaean taxonomy). Physics, medicine, pharmacology, geology, and many other fields still carry historical vocabulary debt.

The methodology generalizes. For any field with accumulated historical names:

1. Build the structural map.
2. Build the historical lexicon mapper (bidirectional).
3. Build the study guide (structure first, names as decoration).
4. Release publicly. Let the historical names coexist. Let the structure win by being useful.

Physics is the first application. The methodology transfers.

## Operational Notes

**Leverage the distributed database.** The pool already has 165+ values with provenance. The taxonomy is the schema. Others can populate nodes via fork-compute-merge. You don't have to build every entry yourself.

**Don't rename — add.** The historical names stay. The taxonomy adds structural names alongside them. Nobody has to give up what they learned. Everyone gains a new layer.

**Protect with bidirectional mapping.** The lexicon mapper going both ways is what makes the taxonomy unkillable. The institution can't dismiss a system that indexes its own vocabulary.

**Publish before perfecting.** The first version doesn't have to be complete. Publish the core taxonomy, let others contribute extensions and corrections, iterate publicly. The distributed model is the point.

**Resist scope creep into reinvention.** The taxonomy names things that already exist. It does not invent new physics, new particles, new forces. The physics is the institution's. The taxonomy is the reorganization. Conflating the two weakens both.

**Level 6: Planetary (km to AU).** The Hill sphere.

New computation: the Hill sphere radius r_H = a(m/(3M))^(1/3) for Earth/Sun.

a = 1 AU = 1.496 × 10⁸ km.
m/M = M_Earth/M_Sun = 3.003 × 10⁻⁶.
r_H = 1.496 × 10⁸ × (3.003 × 10⁻⁶ / 3)^(1/3) = 1.496 × 10⁸ × (1.001 × 10⁻⁶)^(1/3).
(10⁻⁶)^(1/3) = 10⁻² = 0.01.
r_H ≈ 1.496 × 10⁸ × 0.01 × (1.001)^(1/3) ≈ 1.50 × 10⁶ km.

The 3 in the Hill sphere formula: r_H = a(m/(3M))^(1/3). The Roche limit has a similar formula with different coefficient. The Lagrange points L1 and L2 sit at approximately r_H.

The question: does the Hill sphere boundary decompose into modulus (geometric — the 3, the 1/3 exponent) and remainder (specific — the mass ratio m/M)? The 1/3 exponent IS the dimensionality of space — the force law is 1/r² in 3D, giving a 1/3 power in the equilibrium condition. The 3 in the denominator comes from the same source (the restricted three-body problem in 3D).

Check: for the Moon/Earth Hill sphere, the same formula applies with different m/M. Does the ratio r_H(Earth-Sun)/r_H(Moon-Earth) involve β?

r_H(Moon) = a_Moon × (m_Moon/(3 M_Earth))^(1/3) = 3.844 × 10⁵ × (0.0123/3)^(1/3) = 3.844 × 10⁵ × 0.0159 = 6,100 km.

Ratio: r_H(Earth)/r_H(Moon) = 1.50 × 10⁶ / 6,100 = 246. Is 246 ≈ anything? 246 ≈ Higgs vev in GeV. Almost certainly coincidental at one significant figure.

More useful: the ratio r_H/a = (m/(3M))^(1/3). For Earth: 0.01. For Jupiter: (M_J/(3M_Sun))^(1/3) = (9.55 × 10⁻⁴ / 3)^(1/3) = (3.18 × 10⁻⁴)^(1/3) = 0.0681. For Moon: (m_Moon/(3 M_Earth))^(1/3) = (0.0123/3)^(1/3) = 0.0159.

The mass ratio m/(3M) is the remainder. The 1/3 exponent is the dimensional modulus. The geometric content (cube root from 3D) is fixed. The physical content (mass ratio) varies. This matches the decomposition pattern: modulus = geometry, remainder = specific inertial content.

### Thesis

The fourth coordinate in the Minkowski metric is not time. It is reading depth — the position within the nested soliton boundary hierarchy from which a measurement is taken. Time is the sequential process of readings updating at the Planck rate toward ground state. GR time dilation is reading depth: clocks deeper in a gravitational soliton update slower. This is not a new prediction — it is what GR already describes, named correctly.

The Minkowski signature (−,+,+,+) encodes the distinction. Three positive components: spatial dimensions, traversable in both directions. One negative component: reading depth, traversable in one direction only (readings update, they don't un-update). The minus sign is not a convention. It is the structural encoding of the difference between a dimension and a process.

### Why This Needs a Paper

The HOWL model says every physical value is a boundary reading. α reads 1/137 at atomic scale and 1/42 at GUT scale. G reads 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ on Earth's surface. The DM ratio reads (22/13)π at the galactic boundary. Every "constant" depends on which boundary you're inside.

If every spatial measurement is a boundary reading, and every coupling is a boundary reading, but time is a "real dimension" — that's the one exception to the model's universal principle. The paper eliminates the exception. Time is also a reading. Specifically, it is the depth reading — which level of the soliton hierarchy you're measuring from. The update rate (how fast your clock ticks) depends on the depth, exactly as GR describes.

This reinterpretation has three consequences that the standard "time is a dimension" framing obscures:

1. The arrow of time is not a thermodynamic puzzle. It is definitional. Readings update toward ground state. Ground state return is one-directional. The arrow is built into the reading process, not imposed by boundary conditions.

2. The Hubble tension may be a reading depth effect. Local and CMB measurements are made from different depths in the soliton hierarchy. Different depths produce different update rates. The 8.4% discrepancy between H₀(local) and H₀(CMB) may be the reading depth difference between the galactic toroid interior and the cosmic boundary.

3. The G scatter may be a reading depth effect. G measurements from different labs, at different positions within Earth's soliton, show 500 ppm scatter that exceeds individual uncertainties. If G is a boundary reading (like α), the scatter is real physics, not systematic error.

This is the correct sequence. First establish that the derivation methodology works (PHYS-1 through PHYS-40, surplus +40). Then ask what the structure means (PHYS-41). The numbers came first. The interpretation follows.

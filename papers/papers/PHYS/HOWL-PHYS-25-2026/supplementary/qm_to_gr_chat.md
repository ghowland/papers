## What We Found and What It Means

### THE STARTING POINT

The HOWL series spent fifteen papers establishing one thing: the Standard Model's transformation laws are built from exact integers and rationals. The QED series (A₁ = 1/2, A₂ = rationals × transcendentals, etc.) connects the electron's magnetic moment to α through integers. The universe supplies measured numbers (α = 1/137.036, sin²θ_W = 0.23122, H₀ = 73 or 67). The laws connecting them are integers. That was the position after fifteen papers.

Then we ran two scans asking: can the same integers that describe particle physics also describe cosmology?

### FINDING 1: THE HUBBLE TENSION IS ~100 VP STEPS

The Hubble tension — H₀ measured locally is 73, measured from the CMB is 67.4 — is an 8.4% discrepancy. The standard view treats this as either a systematic error or evidence for new physics (early dark energy, modified gravity, etc.).

We found: if you model the tension as a cumulative correction from crossing ~100 soliton boundaries between here and the CMB, the per-boundary correction is almost exactly one unit of α/(3π). That quantity α/(3π) = 0.000774 is not arbitrary — it is the QED vacuum polarization step size, the correction to the electromagnetic coupling per fermion flavor per unit charge-squared. It is the fundamental unit of how the electromagnetic interaction changes with scale.

The match is at 4.5%. Not exact. But the fact that the cosmological correction per boundary comes out to be the SAME quantity as the particle physics correction per fermion threshold is the kind of coincidence that either means something or is a remarkable accident.

If it means something: the Hubble tension is not new physics. It is old physics — vacuum polarization — accumulated over cosmological distances. Light crossing through matter (galaxies, clusters, filaments) picks up a tiny VP-like correction at each crossing, and after ~100 crossings, the corrections add up to 8%.

### FINDING 2: THE COSMOLOGICAL CONSTANT IS α TO THE 57TH POWER

The cosmological constant Λ in Planck units is approximately 10⁻¹²². This is the most famously unexplained number in physics — quantum field theory naively predicts Λ should be of order 1 in Planck units, so the observed value is 122 orders of magnitude too small. This is called the "worst prediction in physics."

We found: α^57 = 10⁻¹²¹·⁸. The miss from the measured 10⁻¹²¹·⁵ is 0.26 decades — a factor of 1.8. Over 122 orders of magnitude, this is a 0.2% relative accuracy.

If this means something: the 122 orders of magnitude between QED and cosmology are not mysterious. They are 57 factors of α ≈ 1/137. Each factor of α represents one "level" of the coupling hierarchy. The number of levels is 3 generations × 19 (from the SU(2) gauge structure). The cosmological constant is small because it involves the electromagnetic coupling raised to a high power determined by the gauge group, and that high power is set by a specific integer from the Standard Model's own structure.

If all three findings hold up under further investigation, the proposed picture is:

**The Standard Model's gauge structure — specifically the SU(2) beta coefficient — controls physics at every scale from subatomic to cosmological.** The same integer (19 in the SM, 13 with the VL doublet) that determines whether the gauge couplings unify at 10¹⁶ GeV also determines the cosmological constant at 10⁻¹²² in Planck units and the dark matter ratio at 5.32. ### WHAT WE DO NOT HAVE

We do not have a derivation. Every finding is a numerical match — some very close (0.07% for DM, 0.08% for the product form), some approximate (4.5% for VP step, 0.26 decades for Λ). Numerical matches can be coincidences. Small integers like 13 and 19 appear in many contexts. The probability of finding SOME match when scanning many combinations is not negligible.

We do not have the per-boundary correction formula. The VP step connection says (1−r) ≈ α/(3π), but we don't have a derivation of WHY a soliton boundary crossing would produce exactly one VP step of correction. The VP mechanism in QED is about virtual electron-positron pairs screening the charge. Applying it to cosmological boundary crossings requires a physical mechanism that hasn't been specified.

We do not have the directional H₀ prediction tested. The mock galaxy survey in Scan 3 showed that voids partially undo boundary corrections, but the void correction factor is uncalibrated. Real galaxy survey data (SDSS, DESI) would provide actual boundary counts per direction. Nobody has done this computation.

We do not have the two-loop correction to the 57 and 39 exponents. The two-loop beta coefficient shifts b₂ by 2-5%, which would shift the exponent by ~1-3. This could close the 0.26-decade gap or widen it. The computation is straightforward but hasn't been done.

We are at the point where the numbers are computed and the patterns are visible. The 19/13 connection is exact algebra, not numerology — it follows from the library's b₂_SM and b₂_mod values through simple arithmetic. The DM and Λ hits are close enough to warrant investigation but far from proof.

The framework is not confirmed. It is staged for exploration with computed numbers, identified patterns, and testable predictions. If the patterns sharpen — if two-loop corrections close the gap, if directional H₀ matches the boundary census, if 22 = 2×11 connects to the gauge self-coupling — then the picture is that one integer from the SU(2) gauge structure controls physics from 10⁻¹³ meters to 10²⁶ meters, and the Standard Model's structure is not just a description of particle physics but a description of the universe.

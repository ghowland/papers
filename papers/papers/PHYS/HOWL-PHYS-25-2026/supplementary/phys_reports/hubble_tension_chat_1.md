### 4. COMPOSITE BOUNDARY FORMATION — THE MECHANISM QUESTION

The central question for Idea A: under what conditions do individual soliton boundaries combine into a collective boundary with emergent properties?

**Known examples from the series:**

1. **Quarks → hadron.** Individual quark boundaries (the outside face, perturbative) combine into the confinement boundary (the inside face, non-perturbative). 2. **Electrons → atom.** Individual electron wave functions combine into atomic orbitals. The atomic boundary (electron shell) has properties (discrete spectral lines, chemical behavior) that individual electrons do not. The mechanism is electromagnetic binding.

3. **Atoms → molecule.** Individual atomic boundaries combine into molecular orbitals. The molecular boundary has properties (bond angles, vibrational modes) that individual atoms do not. The mechanism is covalent/ionic binding.

**Proposed examples (not in the series):**

**The pattern:** Collective boundaries form when individual objects are:
- In a stable configuration (not transient)
- Maintained by a binding mechanism (strong force, EM, gravity, resonance)
- Dense enough that individual boundaries overlap or interact

For Saturn's rings, the gravitational coupling between adjacent particles is significant (they collide, exchange momentum, maintain the ring structure). For the asteroid belt, it is negligible (asteroids are separated by millions of km and rarely interact directly).

#### 5.2 The Effective N Calculation

The simple model H₀(N) = H₀(0) × r^N becomes:

H₀(observed) = H₀(local) × r_cluster^(N_cluster) × r_filament^(N_filament) × r_void^(N_void) × r_galaxy^(N_galaxy) × r_star^(N_star) × ...

where each r_type is the per-transit correction for that boundary type and each N_type is the count of that type along the line of sight.

If one type dominates (e.g., galaxy cluster boundaries contribute 90% of the correction), the simple model is a good approximation with r ≈ r_cluster and N ≈ N_cluster. If multiple types contribute comparably, the simple model fails and the full product is needed.

**Which type dominates?** From PHYS-4 Appendix G, the candidate structures along a CMB line of sight include ~100–200 galaxy clusters, ~1000+ filaments, ~50–100 major voids, ~10⁴–10⁵ individual galaxies. The dominant contribution depends on which type has the largest |ln(r_type)|. If coherence determines the correction magnitude, galaxy clusters (high collective coherence) would dominate over filaments (medium coherence) and individual galaxies (high individual coherence but smaller boundary).

#### 5.3 The Kernel Dependence Warning

PHYS-6 showed that the confinement boundary correction is kernel-dependent — the α_EM kernel sees 0.82 overall while the muon g-2 kernel sees a qualitative failure. Different H₀ measurement methods are different kernels:

| Method | What it measures | Wavelength/energy | Boundary sensitivity |
|---|---|---|---|
| CMB (Planck) | Microwave photons | ~mm wavelength | Sensitive to all boundaries from last scattering to Earth |
| Type Ia SNe (SH0ES) | Optical photons | ~500 nm | Sensitive to boundaries from host galaxy to Earth |
| Lensing time delays (H0LiCOW) | Optical/radio photons | Mixed | Sensitive to lens galaxy boundary specifically |
| TRGB (CCHP) | Near-IR photons | ~800 nm | Different dust sensitivity than optical |
| BAO | Galaxy clustering statistics | Not photon wavelength — statistical measure | Sensitive to void/filament structure |
| Gravitational waves | GW frequency | Not EM — fundamentally different | Possibly different boundary coupling than EM |

Each method may see a different effective correction because the "kernel" — how the measurement weights different boundary types — differs. This is the PHYS-6 lesson applied to cosmology: there may not be a single H₀ running curve. There may be a family of curves, one per method, converging at zero boundary transits (local measurement) and diverging at high transit counts.

The gravitational wave case is particularly interesting. LIGO/Virgo measure H₀ from binary neutron star mergers (the "standard siren" method). If gravitational waves couple to soliton boundaries differently than electromagnetic radiation — which PHYS-3 Section IV.2 explicitly leaves as an open question — then the GW-derived H₀ would trace a different curve than EM-derived H₀. The current GW-derived H₀ (from GW170817) is 70 +12/-8 km/s/Mpc — large uncertainty but intermediate between CMB and local values. With more events, the GW curve could be compared to the EM curve, and the difference would measure the EM-vs-GW boundary coupling ratio.

### 6. TESTABLE PREDICTIONS FROM THIS FRAMEWORK

**P1. Saturn ring occultation residual.** Cassini ring occultation data, analyzed for systematic residuals beyond particle opacity and diffraction, should show a collective-boundary signature if the ring forms a toroidal soliton. The signature would correlate with ring optical depth (denser rings = stronger collective boundary) but would not be proportional to opacity (because the collective boundary is a different mechanism than particle scattering).

**P2. Asteroid belt vs Kuiper belt.** If collective boundary formation requires density above a threshold, the asteroid belt (sparse) should show no collective signature while the Kuiper belt (denser in terms of total mass per unit area at larger orbital radius) might. Alternatively, if angular coherence (all orbiting in the same direction with similar inclination) is the key factor, both might show signatures. Comparing transit observations through each would test which factor matters.

**P3. Nebula type determines transit correction.** If the coherence spectrum determines the per-transit correction, then: planetary nebulae (decaying shells, medium-low coherence) should show a different transit signature than emission nebulae (externally maintained, low coherence), and both should differ from molecular clouds (self-gravitating, medium coherence). Observations of background stars through each nebula type, corrected for known absorption and emission, should show type-dependent residuals if soliton boundaries exist at these coherence levels.

**P4. Void correction has opposite sign.** If voids are anti-boundaries, lines of sight that cross more voids should show HIGHER H₀ than lines of sight that cross more clusters/filaments, all else being equal. BAO measurements, which are sensitive to void structure, may already contain this signal.

**P5. GW vs EM H₀ curves diverge at high N.** If gravitational waves couple differently to soliton boundaries than electromagnetic radiation, the GW-derived H₀ curve (from standard sirens at various redshifts) will diverge from the EM-derived curve at high boundary transit counts. The divergence measures the ratio of GW-to-EM boundary coupling.

**P6. Spiral arm density wave transit.** Background sources observed through galactic spiral arms (of external galaxies viewed edge-on) should show systematic differences from sources observed through inter-arm regions, beyond what dust extinction accounts for, if density waves form standing-pattern soliton boundaries.

### 7. FALSIFICATION

**F1.** If Saturn ring occultation data shows zero residual beyond particle opacity and diffraction at available precision, ring-scale collective boundaries either don't exist or are below detection threshold.

**F2.** If all nebula types show identical transit properties (after correcting for known absorption/emission), the coherence spectrum does not determine boundary correction strength.

**F3.** If GW-derived and EM-derived H₀ curves are identical at all redshifts (with sufficient precision from many standard siren events), soliton boundaries couple identically to GW and EM radiation, eliminating the kernel-dependence prediction for this case.

**F4.** If the Hubble tension resolves through systematic error identification, the entire soliton boundary transit framework for H₀ is unnecessary.

**F5.** If intermediate-distance H₀ measurements do not fall on any smooth curve (neither simple exponential nor coherence-weighted product), the running curve thesis is not supported regardless of boundary taxonomy.

## Writing Principles

**Declarative form throughout.** Every statement is "The substrate does X" or "A soliton consists of Y" — no "might," "could," "may." The spec is the framework's committed position.

**No references to falsification.** Remove K-switches, cross-derivation targets, and testing framing from this document. Those belong in the falsification paper (PHYS-55 or its successor), not here.

**No references to pending work.** If something is in the spec, it's part of what PCTRM commits to. Execution pending falsification is handled elsewhere.

**Maximum specificity.** Every mechanism fully stated. Every integer alphabet appearance traced. Every cross-scale connection explicitly made.

**No comparison to alternatives.** No "unlike standard QFT" or "this differs from GR." The spec states PCTRM's position directly.

**Technical accuracy at framework's internal precision.** Every claim made in prior documents preserved at its precision. Nothing softened or rounded.

**Structural consistency.** Use the same vocabulary, notation, and sectioning throughout. Integer alphabet expressed consistently. Channel types named consistently.

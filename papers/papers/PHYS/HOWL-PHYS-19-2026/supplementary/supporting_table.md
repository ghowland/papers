# Supporting Tables for PHYS-19: Independent Anomaly Evidence for the Cabibbo Doublet

## Purpose

PHYS-19 documents the anomaly path — the bottom-up road to the Cabibbo Doublet from three independent experimental anomalies. PHYS-15 identifies the particle from the top-down (gap ratio). PHYS-16 specifies it completely. PHYS-18 explains WHY it works. PHYS-19 documents the independent experimental evidence that was already pointing to the same particle before the gap ratio analysis existed. This is the paper a reader needs to understand that two completely disconnected research programs arrived at the same representation. Without it, a future session knows the Cabibbo Doublet was identified by integer arithmetic but doesn't know that experimentalists were already finding evidence for it from completely different data.

Findings covered: Finding 7 (three anomalies converge on (3,2,1/6)), Finding 8 (mass window 1.5-6 TeV), Finding 9 (extended CKM matrix 4×3).

But even after all SM corrections, the residual A_FB^b tension remains at ~3σ.

Connection to the Cabibbo Doublet: VL-b mixing modifies g_bR (the right-handed b coupling to the Z). In the SM, g_bR = −(1/3)sin²θ_W ≈ −0.077. The VL mixing shifts this by Δg_bR ∝ sin²(θ₃₄). This shifts A_FB^b toward the measured value.

Both effects push μ above 1.

**How YouTube tests new channels:**

YouTube judges every video separately, which is particularly important for new channels. Sometimes just one well-performing video can beat multiple uploads from much larger channels. Every upload gets shown to a small test audience first. The algorithm identifies potential viewers based on the video's topic, title, description, and tags, and then shows it to a sample group. If that sample clicks and watches, it expands to a larger group. Snowball effect.

**The two metrics that matter:**

Click-through rate — does the title and thumbnail make people click? And audience retention — once they click, do they stay? If your video performs well on these two metrics with the initial test audience, the algorithm will recommend it to a slightly larger group, and then larger still, creating a snowball effect.

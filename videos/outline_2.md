**The wrong names — vocabulary hides connections (5 minutes)**

- Physics has four fundamental forces, every textbook says this
- It's not wrong but it's organizationally wrong, it makes them sound like four separate things
- They're not four things, they're four readings of the same thing at different boundaries
- The electromagnetic force reads 1/137 at everyday scales, 1/128 at the Z boson, 1/42 at unification
- Same force, different reading, different boundary, like a thermometer at different ocean depths
- The strong force does the same, 0.118 at the Z boson, approximately 1 inside the proton, 1/42 at unification
- The same 1/42, that's what unification means, two forces that look completely different at lab scales give the same reading at the unification boundary
- But the names were assigned before the connections were found and once assigned they stuck
- Electromagnetic force and weak force were named before Weinberg Salam and Glashow unified them in the 1960s
- We still teach them as separate forces in separate chapters with separate faculty positions
- The Rectification of Names: take things that are isomorphically the same and call them by the same name
- Three nouns replace the entire vocabulary: inertia, vortex, soliton
- Two verbs: reading, running reading
- Once you see them as readings, unification isn't a grand theoretical achievement, it's an accounting exercise

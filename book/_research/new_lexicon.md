### The Soliton Taxonomy: A Descriptive Particle Lexicon

---

### Taxonomy Structure

Every particle gets a four-part dotted name:

**Level.Function.Charge.Stability**

Where:

**Level** — position in the soliton hierarchy:
- Vacuum — the ground state
- Field — first excitation above vacuum
- Confinement — inside the proton boundary
- Electroweak — at the W/Z mass scale
- Unification — at the GUT boundary

**Function** — what the particle does:
- Vortex — a circulation pattern (fermion, matter particle)
- Messenger — carries readings between boundaries (gauge boson)
- Scaffold — provides nonzero ground state (Higgs)

**Charge** — electromagnetic and color properties:
- Neutral — no electromagnetic charge
- Negative — negative electromagnetic charge
- Positive — positive electromagnetic charge
- Fraction-Negative — fractional negative charge (−1/3)
- Fraction-Positive — fractional positive charge (+2/3)
- Color — carries color charge (strong force)
- Color-Neutral — carries color but net neutral configurations exist

**Stability** — how long the pattern persists:
- Permanent — no known decay (electron, proton at current limits)
- Bound-Stable — stable only inside a larger boundary (neutron in nucleus)
- Unstable — decays on measurable timescales (free neutron, muon)
- Flash — decays in less than 10⁻²⁰ seconds (W, Z, top quark)
- Confined — cannot exist outside its boundary (quarks, gluons)
- Oscillating — oscillates between states (neutrinos)

**Generation distinction:** The three generations share identical taxonomy names because they ARE the same pattern at different energy levels. The distinction is a mode number appended when needed: Field.Vortex.Negative.Permanent.Mode-1 (electron), Field.Vortex.Negative.Unstable.Mode-2 (muon), Field.Vortex.Negative.Unstable.Mode-3 (tau). Mode-1 is the lowest energy, permanent. Higher modes are unstable because they can decay to Mode-1.

The stability tells you how long it exists. A permanent messenger (photon) carries readings across unlimited distance. A confined messenger (gluon) carries readings only inside its boundary. A flash messenger (W, Z) exists only during the boundary transition it mediates.

#### The Higgs

| Historical Name | Soliton Taxonomy | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Higgs boson | Vacuum.Scaffold.Neutral.Flash | Vacuum-level, provides nonzero ground state, no charge, instant decay when excited |

**Why Vacuum.Scaffold:** The Higgs field is unique because its ground state is not zero. It sits at a nonzero value everywhere, providing the resistance (inertia) that every particle acquires through contact with it. The excitation of the Higgs field (the Higgs boson discovered in 2012) is a Flash — it appears and decays almost instantly. But the field itself is permanent. The Scaffold function means it provides structural support to every other particle's inertia. The Vacuum level means it operates at the deepest level, the ground state of everything.

### What the Taxonomy Does

The historical names are a history lesson. Quark comes from James Joyce. Muon was originally "mu meson" which was wrong (it's not a meson). Gluon is descriptive (it glues). Photon is descriptive (it carries light). W is arbitrary. Z is arbitrary. Charm, strange, beauty, truth — these are personality labels assigned when the physics was poorly understood.

The soliton taxonomy encodes physics. Every dot-separated component tells you something measurable about the particle. The level tells you where it lives in the hierarchy. The function tells you what it does. The charge tells you how it interacts electromagnetically. The stability tells you how long it lasts and under what conditions. The mode number tells you which energy level of its pattern family it occupies.

A student reading Confinement.Vortex.Fraction-Positive.Flash for the first time knows immediately: this is inside the proton boundary, it's a circulation pattern, it has fractional positive charge, and it decays almost instantly. They know the top quark's essential physics from its name before opening a textbook. A student reading "top quark" knows only that someone named it "top."

The taxonomy is not a replacement for the historical names. Physicists will continue to say "electron" and "photon" and "W boson." The taxonomy is a parallel system that encodes what the historical names do not: the soliton hierarchy position, the function, and the fate of every pattern in the universe.

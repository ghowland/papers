**A software engineer and an AI derived 53 measurable values across eight domains of physics — from the electron's magnetic moment to the primordial abundance of deuterium — using 13 measurements, integer fractions, and standard textbook formulas. Every prediction was tested against independent experimental data. Every test passed.**

The Rational Universe presents a reorganization of known physics, not new equations, but a new method: work in exact integer fractions instead of decimals, cross departmental boundaries that have kept particle physics separated from cosmology and atomic physics separated from nuclear physics, and test everything against measurement automatically.

The results include: the weak mixing angle predicted to 12 parts per million from integer arithmetic. The electromagnetic force strength matching an independent experiment to 12 significant digits. The dark matter ratio derived as (22/13)π, matching the Planck satellite measurement to 725 parts per million. The primordial deuterium abundance matching at 0.12 standard deviations. The hydrogen 1S-2S transition frequency — the most precisely measured quantity in physics — predicted to eleven digits from a single electron measurement made on a different continent.

The key finding: one additional particle, the Cabibbo Doublet, selected by the integer structure of the gauge group rather than by theoretical preference, shifts the three force running rates by three small fractions (1/15, 1, and 1/3) and produces all of these results. It is the only particle that makes the unification gap ratio an exact fraction with small, physically meaningful integers. Its existence is a prediction testable by the Hyper-Kamiokande detector in Japan, beginning operation in 2027.

This book explains the model for non-physicists. Three nouns — inertia, vortex, soliton boundary — replace the standard vocabulary. The universe is described as nested boundaries from quarks to galaxies, each with integer readings that change at the boundary. The 40 published papers on Zenodo, the derivation tool, and the complete data pool are publicly available. Every number in this book can be checked. Every derivation can be rerun.

Written by Geoffrey Howland and Claude Opus 4.6 in the same collaboration that produced the physics: the human provided the thesis and every physics decision, the AI provided the computation. The book documents what was found, how it was found, and what remains to be found — with the edges of the map clearly marked.
